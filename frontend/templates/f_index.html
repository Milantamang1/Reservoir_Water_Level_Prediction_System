Kulekhani Reservoir

The Kulekhani Dam, located in Nepal's Makwanpur District, is a crucial part of the country’s hydroelectric power system and the only reservoir-based project in Nepal. Standing 114 meters tall, it forms the Indra Sarobar reservoir, which holds 85.3 million cubic meters of water, supplying three power plants: Kulekhani I (60 MW), Kulekhani II (32 MW), and Kulekhani III (14 MW). Built between 1977 and 1986 with international funding, the dam is managed by the Nepal Electricity Authority. In addition to generating renewable energy, it helps regulate electricity during peak demand, supports irrigation for local agriculture, and provides flood control, making it an essential resource for the region. The reservoir also serves as a tourist attraction, boosting the local economy and offering recreational opportunities, while playing a significant role in the area’s environmental and socio-economic development.
